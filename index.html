About the minimum wage

The StatsSA food basket ensures that individuals have enough carbohydrates (energy) and does not look to protein, micronutrients content etc.

This amount was R531 per person per month, as of April 2017.

In early 2014 PACSA, in consultation with a registered dietician, formulated a Minimum Nutritional Food Basket. This basket provides data on which one can start talking realistically about adequacies in wages and ensures that current food expenditure patterns are not conflated with the food expenditure required to secure a nutritional basket of food.

To obtain a diet complete in minimum nutrition (assuming a 10 500 kJ (2 509 kcal) daily intake) would cost R621.35 per person per month, as of August 2017.
